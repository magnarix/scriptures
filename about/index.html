About the Ministry

Scriptures.us is the public home of the theological and scriptural ministry of Rev. Basilius Magnus. It exists to make available, in written form, the work of a ministry that proceeds from one conviction above all others: that Sacred Scripture, received within the living tradition of the Church and illuminated by her sacramental and theological life, remains the inexhaustible ground of Christian faith, thought, and proclamation.

This is not a publishing platform, a devotional resource, or an academic project—though it may at times share certain qualities with each. It is the written extension of an active ministry of preaching, teaching, retreat direction, and theological reflection. What appears here has, in most cases, first been spoken to a congregation, taught to a gathering, or worked out in the sustained discipline of prayer and study. It is offered to a wider readership not because the internet demands content, but because the Word of God, once proclaimed, does not belong to its proclaimer alone.

The theological character of this ministry is rooted in the scriptural, patristic, and sacramental traditions of the Church. It reads Scripture as the Church has always read it: as a living word addressed to the faithful in every age, demanding the full resources of exegesis, theology, and contemplative attention. It takes seriously the witness of the Church Fathers, the theological heritage of the councils, and the ongoing work of the Spirit in the life of the Church. It proceeds with the conviction that reverence and intellectual rigor are not in tension but are, when rightly ordered, aspects of the same fidelity.

The writing gathered here tends toward density rather than brevity, toward careful articulation rather than summary, and toward the kind of theological prose that asks the reader to slow down rather than scan. This is deliberate. The texts of Sacred Scripture were written to be dwelt in, not consumed. The reflections, homilies, and studies published here attempt to honor that quality by refusing to simplify what is not simple and by attending to what the text actually says before rushing to what it might mean for us.

Rev. Basilius Magnus is an ordained minister whose work spans preaching, theological writing, parish ministry, retreat direction, and teaching. Inquiries regarding preaching engagements, retreat work, or other forms of ministry may be directed through the Contact page.
